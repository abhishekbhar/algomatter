# Kill Switch & Safety

When you are trading with real capital, things can go wrong — bugs, flash crashes, unexpected market conditions. Algomatter provides multiple layers of safety to protect your capital, with the kill switch as the most important one.

## What the kill switch does

The kill switch is an emergency stop mechanism that:

1. **Immediately stops** your strategy from placing any new orders.
2. **Cancels all pending orders** on the exchange.
3. **Optionally liquidates** all open positions at market price.

Think of it as pulling the emergency brake. Everything stops.

## Triggering the kill switch

### From the UI

Click the red **Kill Switch** button on the Live Trading dashboard. A confirmation dialog will appear asking whether you also want to liquidate open positions:

- **Stop only:** The strategy halts but your positions remain open. You manage them manually.
- **Stop and liquidate:** The strategy halts and all open positions are closed at market price immediately.

### From the API

You can also trigger the kill switch programmatically:

```bash
curl -X POST https://api.algomatter.com/v1/deployments/{deployment_id}/kill \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"liquidate": true}'
```

This is useful if you want to integrate the kill switch with external monitoring tools or scripts.

## Automatic kill switch triggers

You don't have to watch your screen 24/7. Algomatter can trigger the kill switch automatically based on rules you configure.

### Maximum drawdown limit

Set a percentage threshold. If your equity drops by that amount from its highest point, the kill switch fires automatically.

**Example:** Starting equity is $10,000. Equity peaks at $12,000, then drops. With a 15% max drawdown limit, the kill switch triggers if equity falls to $10,200 (15% below the $12,000 peak).

Configure this under **Deployment Settings → Risk Management → Max Drawdown**.

### Maximum daily loss

Set a dollar amount or percentage that you are willing to lose in a single day. If the limit is hit, the strategy pauses until the next UTC day.

**Example:** With a $500 daily loss limit, if your strategy loses $500 in a day, it stops placing orders until midnight UTC.

Configure this under **Deployment Settings → Risk Management → Daily Loss Limit**.

### Maximum consecutive losses

Set the number of consecutive losing trades allowed before the strategy pauses. This catches strategies that may be stuck in a bad loop.

**Example:** With a limit of 5 consecutive losses, the strategy pauses after 5 losing trades in a row and sends you a notification.

> Start with conservative limits. A 10-15% max drawdown and a 3-5% daily loss limit are reasonable defaults for most strategies.

## Position liquidation behavior

When the kill switch fires with liquidation enabled:

1. All open positions are closed with **market orders**.
2. Orders are sent immediately to the exchange — there is no delay or batching.
3. Market orders ensure execution but may experience slippage during volatile conditions.
4. The liquidation status is logged in the trade log with a `[KILL SWITCH]` tag.

If you prefer to manage exits yourself, trigger the kill switch with `"liquidate": false` and handle positions manually on the exchange.

## Restarting after a kill switch

After a kill switch event:

1. The strategy enters a **stopped** state. It will not restart automatically.
2. Review the trade log and strategy logs to understand what happened.
3. Check your exchange account directly to confirm all positions and orders are in the expected state.
4. If the trigger was a false alarm or a temporary condition, you can redeploy the strategy from the Strategies page.

> Never immediately restart a strategy after a kill switch without understanding why it triggered. Take time to review the situation.

## Other safety features

### Rate limiting

Algomatter limits the number of orders a strategy can place per minute to prevent runaway trading. The default limit is 10 orders per minute. If your strategy attempts to exceed this, additional orders are queued and a warning is logged.

### Position size limits

Set a maximum position size (in quote currency or base quantity) that your strategy can hold. Any order that would exceed this limit is rejected.

Configure under **Deployment Settings → Risk Management → Max Position Size**.

### Duplicate order prevention

Algomatter detects and blocks duplicate orders — for example, if a bug causes your strategy to submit the same buy order multiple times within seconds.

### Exchange connectivity monitoring

Algomatter continuously monitors connectivity to your exchange. If the connection drops:

- The strategy pauses automatically.
- You receive a notification.
- When connectivity is restored, the strategy resumes (or stays paused if you prefer — configurable in settings).

## Notification settings

Configure how you want to be alerted about safety events:

- **Email:** Receive an email when the kill switch triggers, drawdown limits are approached, or connectivity issues occur.
- **Dashboard:** All events appear in the notification center within the Algomatter app.

Enable notifications under **Settings → Notifications**.

## Best practices

1. **Always configure the kill switch** before going live. Do not rely on manual monitoring alone.
2. **Set max drawdown below your pain threshold.** If a 20% loss would cause you to panic, set the limit at 15%.
3. **Test your kill switch.** Trigger it once during paper trading to verify it works as expected.
4. **Use multiple safety layers.** Combine max drawdown with daily loss limits and position size caps.
5. **Review triggers, don't ignore them.** A kill switch trigger is a signal that something is off. Investigate before redeploying.
