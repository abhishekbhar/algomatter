# Going Live

Going live means your strategy will place real orders on a real exchange with real money. This guide covers everything you need to do before, during, and after flipping the switch.

## Prerequisites checklist

Before deploying to live, make sure you have completed each of these steps:

- [ ] **Backtest passes:** Your strategy is profitable across multiple date ranges with realistic [slippage and commission](/docs/backtesting/slippage-commission) settings.
- [ ] **Paper trading period:** You have run the strategy in [paper trading](/docs/trading/paper-trading) for at least 2 weeks and results are consistent with the backtest.
- [ ] **Exchange connected:** Your exchange broker is set up and tested in the [Brokers](/docs/getting-started/connecting-exchange) page.
- [ ] **Sufficient balance:** Your exchange account has enough funds for the strategy's position sizing.
- [ ] **Risk limits set:** You have configured the [kill switch](/docs/trading/kill-switch) and maximum drawdown limits.

> Do not skip the paper trading step. Even experienced quant developers paper trade new strategies before going live.

## Deploying to live

1. Open your strategy in **Strategies → Hosted**.
2. Click **Deploy** in the top toolbar.
3. Select **Live Trading**.
4. Configure the deployment:
   - **Symbol:** Trading pair (e.g., BTC/USDT)
   - **Timeframe:** Candle interval
   - **Broker:** Select your exchange connection
   - **Capital allocation:** How much of your exchange balance the strategy can use
5. Review the confirmation dialog carefully — it shows the symbol, exchange, and capital at risk.
6. Click **Confirm and Start**.

Your strategy is now live. Orders will be placed on your exchange within seconds of each candle close.

## Monitoring live positions

The **Live Trading** dashboard gives you real-time visibility:

### Key metrics to watch

- **Current position:** What you are holding right now, including unrealized P&L.
- **Today's P&L:** Profit or loss since midnight UTC.
- **Total P&L:** Cumulative profit or loss since deployment.
- **Equity curve:** Real-time portfolio value chart.

### Trade log

Every order placed by the strategy is logged with:
- Timestamp
- Side (buy/sell)
- Quantity and price
- Fees paid
- Running P&L

### Strategy logs

Messages from `self.log()` appear here. Use these to debug behavior or monitor strategy state.

## Risk management settings

Before or during a live deployment, configure these safety nets:

### Maximum drawdown limit

Set the maximum percentage your equity can drop from its peak before the strategy is automatically stopped. For example, setting this to 15% means the strategy will halt if equity drops 15% from its highest point.

### Maximum daily loss

Set a daily loss limit (absolute or percentage). If reached, the strategy pauses for the rest of the day and resumes the next trading day.

### Position size limits

Cap the maximum position size the strategy can hold. This prevents bugs or unexpected conditions from taking oversized positions.

> Start with conservative risk limits. You can always loosen them as you gain confidence. A 10-15% max drawdown limit is a reasonable starting point.

## What to watch for

### First few days

- **Verify order execution:** Check that orders are actually filling on the exchange (not just appearing in the dashboard). Log in to your exchange and confirm.
- **Compare to paper:** Live fills should be similar to paper trading. If you see significantly worse execution, investigate slippage or liquidity issues.
- **Check fees:** Verify the commission rates match your expectations.

### Ongoing monitoring

- **Weekly review:** Look at the equity curve and metrics weekly. Compare to backtest projections.
- **Drawdown alerts:** Algomatter sends notifications when drawdown exceeds thresholds. Pay attention to these.
- **Market regime changes:** A strategy that works in a trending market may struggle in a range-bound one. Be prepared to pause and re-evaluate.

## When to stop a strategy

Stop your strategy if:

- **Drawdown exceeds your comfort level**, even if the kill switch hasn't triggered yet.
- **Results deviate significantly from backtest** and paper trading expectations over multiple weeks.
- **Market conditions change fundamentally** (e.g., a regime shift from trending to choppy).
- **You discover a bug** in the strategy logic.
- **Exchange issues** arise (API errors, connectivity problems, abnormal spreads).

To stop, click the **Stop** button on the Live Trading dashboard. This halts the strategy immediately. Open positions remain on the exchange — you can close them manually or let the next deployment handle them.

## Scaling up

Once a strategy has been running live successfully for several weeks:

1. Increase capital allocation gradually (e.g., 2x, then 3x).
2. Paper trade at the new size first to verify position sizing works correctly.
3. Monitor the first few trades at the new size closely.

Never jump from paper trading straight to your full intended capital. Scale in gradually.
